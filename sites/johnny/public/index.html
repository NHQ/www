Employment History


2003-2006


Before learning to program, I worked for Chicago Public Schools. I taught and subbed first, then moved into supplementary education under the No Child Left Behind act (NCLB). My work took me to almost every CPS School which could be classified as "under-performing". You can imagine what that means in a district like CPS, where nearly 40% or entering students do not graduate. In my role, I interfaced with faculty, administrations, teachers and students at over 100 schools. Eventually this line of work would send me to schools and districts around the Midwest, and down the Mississippi River. The work was saddening and demoralizing, and in my marginalized role, I could do nothing to improve things. This served as inspiration which would eventually make me to realize that I could do more if I learned to code.


2006-Present


Independent contracting and freelance web development have supplemented my education and provided much of my practical experience. I have worked for interactive agencies, eCommerce sites, small business, non-profits, and individuals.
